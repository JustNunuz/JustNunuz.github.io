Don't scan

Random QR Codes

Scanning random QR codes is risky, as they can link to malicious sites, download harmful software, or trigger unauthorized actions on your device. Since QR codes hide their destination website, cybercriminals can use them to direct you to phishing pages that steal credentials and sensitive information.

Learn more

Dangers of scanning random QR Codes

  • Phishing Attacks: Redirects to fake websites to steal login credentials or financial information.
  • Malware Downloads: Triggers downloads of malicious software that can infect your device.
  • Privacy Invasion: Gathers data from your device, potentially selling your information without consent.
